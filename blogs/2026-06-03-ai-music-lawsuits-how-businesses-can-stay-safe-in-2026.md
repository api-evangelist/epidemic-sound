AI music lawsuits are no longer rare in 2026, with major litigation involving platforms like Suno and Udio, plus GEMA's victory against OpenAI and settlements between music platforms and major labels over unauthorized use of copyrighted material for AI training. The article outlines how businesses need clear rights documentation and transparent training data practices, emphasizing that "businesses need the right to use the music, not just generate it."
