A high-angle shot is a filmmaking technique where the camera is positioned above the subject, looking down on them, ranging from subtle angles slightly above eye level to extreme bird's-eye perspectives. Filmmakers use high-angle shots to establish narrative cues, suggest power imbalances between characters, create magical or awe-inspiring moments, and evoke feelings of vulnerability, helplessness, or wonder depending on context and how extreme the angle is.
